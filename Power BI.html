Diving into the annals of history, the 1853 Stockholm cholera outbreak emerges as a haunting tale of resilience and tragedy. Set against the backdrop of Sweden during the third cholera pandemic, this project unveils the harrowing events of that fateful year. With data graciously provided by Quantum Analytics NG, sourced from meticulously handwritten church records, the narrative of this outbreak comes to life.

Navigating through the Swedish language barrier, the dataset was skillfully translated to English using the wonders of technology, courtesy of Google Translate.

What unfolds is a chilling tableau of 248 recorded deaths, revealing a stark reality: 52% female, 48% male – lives lost to the merciless grip of cholera. Yet, amidst the darkness, glimmers of insight emerge. The average age of the departed souls, a mere 34 years, serves as a poignant reminder of the indiscriminate nature of the disease. As the seasons waxed and waned, September cast a grim shadow, boasting the highest mortality rate, while December whispered of respite with its fewer deaths.

Through this analysis, we honor the memory of those lost to history, seeking to shed light on the untold stories of resilience and sorrow that echo through the corridors of time.

Discovering which countries bear the brunt of these encounters, which body parts face the fiercest assaults when the ocean's predators strike most frequently, and which species reign supreme in these perilous encounters – this venture offers insights as captivating as the creatures themselves.
